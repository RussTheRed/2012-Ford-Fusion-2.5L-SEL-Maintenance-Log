- #[[2012 Ford Fusion 3FAHP0JA9CR290609]]
	- Mileage: 259,380
	- Service: Replaced suspected worn tensioner and drive (serpentine) belt.
		- Note: Although previously unmentioned, I replaced the belt about a month prior due to what sounded like slipping. After I installed the new belt, it continued to squeak. TLDR is, the tensioner may not have been needed as I think the first belt I bought was a tad too big and was just slipping again. This is because when I installed the first belt, pulling the tensioner back and slipping the belt over the last guide pulley (Pulley no.6 in the drive belt reference diagram.) was easy. Installing this new belt that I purchased using the proper part number instead of letting AutoZone figure it out, required pulling the tensioner back and removing two bolts from that guide pulley to slip the new belt on.
			- Anti-seize was applied to the bolts on the tensioner as well as all 3 bolts on the guide pulley. There were signs of wear on pulley numbers 7 and 6, so I believe I’ll be in this area again eventually. This endeavor did stop the squeaking though.
